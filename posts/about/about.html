Hebbian Learning

Thoughts and experiments on artificial neural networks

Published on 2 May 2016

Hy, I'm writing this post because I want to share some experiments on artificial neural networks that I'm doing in my free time. I have been using artificial neural networks for about a year now and I successfully used a convolutional neural network to find and recognize cars license plates from images.
Recently I started to think about the differences between biological and artificial neural networks because I believe that the field of artificial intelligence have a lot to learn from the biological nervous system. Although there has been great progress in this field in recent years, there are some big problems yet to solve.

One of the biggest problems of ANN is generalization, infact ANN are pretty bad at generalizing what they learn, you need a lot of data to make them work well. Also they are very very slow at learning new things compared to the Human brain, for example Humans are able to learn to recognizing an object by watching it one or few times while ANN require training on hundreds or thousands of images. I will talk a bit more about this problem later in this post and I will explain why I believe we are able to learns new things so quickly and why current ANN are not able to do that. Another problem in current artificial neural networks is that they can't be extended in an efficient way. For example let's say I want to train an ANN to recognize handwritten digits but I decide to train it only with images of numbers and without images of letters. I will end up with a network that is able to recognize numbers but not letters obviously. Let's say now that I changed my mind and I want to train the same network to recognize letters and numbers, so I train the network with images of letters. This time I end up with an ANN that is able to recognize letters but not numbers infact the problem is that the network overwrite what he had learned before. The common approach is to train the network with all the data at the same time but that it's not the way biological nervous systems works, we are able to learn new things without the need of forgetting other things.
